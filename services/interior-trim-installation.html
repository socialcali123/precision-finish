Custom crown molding, baseboards, and architectural details

Interior Trim Options

Explore our range of custom trim solutions for your home

Crown Molding

Elegant trim installed where walls meet the ceiling, creating a sophisticated transition that adds architectural interest and visual height to any room. Available in various profiles from simple to ornate.

Baseboards

Installed where walls meet the floor, baseboards provide a finished look while protecting walls from damage. Options range from simple, clean profiles to more detailed designs that complement your interior style.

Door & Window Casings

Framing elements that surround doors and windows, adding definition and style. Casings can be simple and understated or elaborate with decorative corner blocks for added visual interest.

Chair Rails

Horizontal molding installed approximately 32-36 inches from the floor, originally designed to protect walls from chair damage. Today, chair rails serve as decorative elements that can define wainscoting or create visual interest.

Picture Rails

Installed higher on the wall than chair rails, picture rails provide a traditional method for hanging artwork without damaging walls. They add period charm to formal spaces and practical functionality.

Coffered Ceilings

Architectural ceiling treatment featuring recessed panels framed by molding, creating a sophisticated, dimensional ceiling that adds luxury and character to dining rooms, libraries, and formal living spaces.

Our Interior Trim Installation Process

A meticulous approach that ensures exceptional results

1

Consultation & Design

We begin with a thorough consultation to understand your vision, preferences, and goals. Our design team will help you select the perfect trim profiles, materials, and finishes to complement your space and achieve your desired aesthetic.

2

Detailed Measurements

We take precise measurements of your space, accounting for all architectural elements, corners, transitions, and other features. This ensures a perfect fit and seamless integration with your existing interior.

3

Material Selection & Preparation

Based on your design choices, we select premium materials that will deliver the desired look and durability. All materials are carefully prepared, with proper acclimation to your home's environment to prevent future issues with expansion or contraction.

4

Precise Cutting & Fitting

Our skilled craftsmen cut each piece of trim with meticulous precision, creating perfect mitered corners and seamless joints. We account for any irregularities in walls or ceilings to ensure a flawless fit throughout your space.

5

Professional Installation

Using proper techniques and tools, we install your trim with careful attention to detail. We secure each piece properly to ensure stability and longevity, while maintaining perfect alignment and spacing throughout the installation.

6

Finishing Touches

Once the trim is installed, we apply finishing touches such as filling nail holes, caulking seams, and sanding any rough edges. This attention to detail ensures a seamless, professional appearance that looks like it's always been part of your home.

7

Painting or Staining

If your trim requires on-site finishing, our expert painters apply premium paints or stains to achieve your desired color and finish. We use proper techniques to ensure even coverage and a beautiful, durable result that enhances your trim's architectural details.

8

Final Inspection & Cleanup

We conduct a thorough inspection to ensure every detail meets our high standards of quality. The work area is completely cleaned, debris is removed, and we walk through the project with you to ensure your complete satisfaction with the finished result.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our interior trim installation services

How long does interior trim installation take?

The timeline varies depending on the scope of the project. A single room with basic crown molding and baseboards might be completed in 1-2 days, while more extensive installations involving multiple rooms or complex designs can take 3-7 days. We'll provide a specific timeline during your consultation based on your project's requirements.

What materials do you use for interior trim?

We work with a variety of high-quality materials including solid wood (pine, oak, maple, poplar), medium-density fiberboard (MDF), polyurethane, and PVC. The best choice depends on your design preferences, budget, and the specific requirements of your space. Our team will help you select the ideal material for your project.

Can you match existing trim in my home?

Yes, we specialize in matching existing trim profiles to maintain consistency throughout your home. Our craftsmen can replicate most trim styles, even if they're no longer commercially available. For historic homes, we take special care to ensure period-appropriate details that preserve your home's architectural integrity.

Should trim be painted before or after installation?

Both approaches have advantages. Pre-painting trim can be more efficient for large projects, while painting after installation ensures that all seams, nail holes, and joints are properly filled and finished. We typically recommend a hybrid approach: applying a primer and base coat before installation, then doing final coats and touch-ups after installation for the most professional result.

How do I maintain my interior trim?

Maintenance is generally simple. For painted trim, regular dusting and occasional cleaning with a mild soap solution is usually sufficient. For stained or natural wood trim, periodic dusting and application of appropriate wood cleaners or polishes will maintain its beauty. We'll provide specific care instructions based on your trim material and finish.
