Wainscoting Styles

Explore our range of custom wainscoting options for your home

Raised Panel

The most traditional and formal style of wainscoting, featuring beveled panels with raised centers. This elegant design adds sophistication to dining rooms, libraries, and formal living spaces.

Beadboard

Featuring vertical planks with distinctive beaded grooves, this charming style adds cottage-inspired character to kitchens, bathrooms, and casual spaces. A versatile option that works in both traditional and contemporary homes.

Board and Batten

A popular style featuring flat panels with vertical battens (strips) placed at regular intervals. This clean, architectural look works beautifully in transitional and modern farmhouse interiors, adding dimension without being overly ornate.

Flat Panel

A more streamlined alternative to raised panel wainscoting, featuring recessed panels with clean lines. This versatile style bridges traditional and contemporary design, making it perfect for transitional homes.

Overlay

A modern interpretation featuring geometric patterns applied directly to flat panels. This contemporary style creates visual interest and can be customized with various patterns to suit your design aesthetic.

Picture Frame

Simple rectangular frames applied to walls create an elegant, understated look. This versatile style works well in both traditional and contemporary settings and can be installed at various heights to achieve different effects.

The Benefits of Wainscoting

Why wainscoting is a valuable addition to your home

Enhanced Aesthetics

Wainscoting adds architectural interest, texture, and dimension that transforms plain walls into elegant design features with timeless appeal.

Increased Home Value

Quality architectural details like wainscoting are desirable features that can significantly increase your property's market value and appeal to potential buyers.

Wall Protection

Wainscoting provides an extra layer of protection against scuffs, dents, and everyday wear, particularly in high-traffic areas like hallways and dining rooms.

Improved Proportions

Strategically placed wainscoting can visually adjust room proportions, making ceilings appear higher or creating more balanced dimensions in awkward spaces.

Conceals Imperfections

Wainscoting is an excellent solution for covering wall imperfections or uneven surfaces without extensive repairs, while adding beauty and character.

Improved Acoustics

The additional layer on your walls can help absorb sound and reduce echo, creating a more comfortable acoustic environment in your home.

Our Wainscoting Installation Process

A meticulous approach that ensures exceptional results

1

Consultation & Design

We begin with a thorough consultation to understand your vision, preferences, and goals. Our design team will help you select the perfect wainscoting style, height, and finish to complement your space and achieve your desired aesthetic.

2

Detailed Measurements

We take precise measurements of your space, accounting for all architectural elements, electrical outlets, switches, and other features. This ensures a perfect fit and seamless integration with your existing interior.

3

Material Selection & Preparation

Based on your design choices, we select premium materials that will deliver the desired look and durability. All materials are carefully prepared, with proper acclimation to your home's environment to prevent future issues with expansion or contraction.

4

Wall Preparation

We thoroughly prepare your walls to ensure a solid foundation for the wainscoting. This includes cleaning, repairing any damage, and ensuring the surface is level. Proper preparation is crucial for a flawless, long-lasting installation.

5

Precise Cutting & Assembly

Our skilled craftsmen cut each component with meticulous precision, creating perfect mitered corners and seamless joints. For panel wainscoting, we carefully assemble the framework and panels to ensure proper alignment and spacing.

6

Professional Installation

Using proper techniques and tools, we install your wainscoting with careful attention to detail. We secure each piece properly to ensure stability and longevity, while maintaining perfect alignment and spacing throughout the installation.

7

Finishing Touches

Once the wainscoting is installed, we apply finishing touches such as filling nail holes, caulking seams, and sanding any rough edges. We pay special attention to the top rail (chair rail) to ensure it's perfectly level and properly finished.

8

Painting or Staining

Our expert painters apply premium paints or stains to achieve your desired color and finish. We use proper techniques to ensure even coverage and a beautiful, durable result that enhances your wainscoting's architectural details.

9

Final Inspection & Cleanup

We conduct a thorough inspection to ensure every detail meets our high standards of quality. The work area is completely cleaned, debris is removed, and we walk through the project with you to ensure your complete satisfaction with the finished result.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our wainscoting installation services

What is the ideal height for wainscoting?

Traditional wainscoting is typically installed at 32-36 inches high (chair rail height), but the ideal height depends on your room's proportions and your design preferences. In rooms with higher ceilings, wainscoting can extend to 48 inches or even higher for a more dramatic effect. During our consultation, we'll recommend the perfect height for your specific space.

What materials do you use for wainscoting?

We work with a variety of high-quality materials including solid wood, medium-density fiberboard (MDF), PVC, and composite materials. The best choice depends on your design preferences, budget, and the specific requirements of your space. MDF is often preferred for painted wainscoting due to its stability and smooth finish, while solid wood is ideal for stained applications.

How long does wainscoting installation take?

The timeline varies depending on the size of the area and complexity of the design. A single room with standard wainscoting might be completed in 2-3 days, while more extensive installations covering multiple rooms or featuring complex designs can take 4-7 days. We'll provide a specific timeline during your consultation based on your project's scope.

Can wainscoting be installed in bathrooms or other moisture-prone areas?

Yes, wainscoting can be installed in bathrooms and other moisture-prone areas when the appropriate materials and finishes are used. For these spaces, we recommend moisture-resistant options like PVC wainscoting, properly sealed wood, or specially treated MDF, along with moisture-resistant paints and sealants to ensure longevity.

Should the wainscoting match my trim color?

Traditionally, wainscoting is painted the same color as your trim for a cohesive look. However, contemporary design offers more flexibility. Wainscoting can be painted in contrasting colors to create a bold statement, or in subtle variations of your wall color for a more nuanced effect. During our consultation, we'll discuss color options that best complement your overall design scheme.
